Objectives


This project aims to integrate, analyze and interactively show the data about the city of New York, focused mainly on students of NYU Stern School of Business, students will be able to evaluate and classify the 59 habitable districts of New York.


The classification and comparison consists of three decision parameters:

-Security

-Distance to NYU Stern School of Business

-Affordability


Graph:

In this section you will find three graphs distributed by the five boroughs of New York. In blue you have Manhattan, in purple to the Bronx, in red to Brooklyn, in yellow to Queens and finally in green to Staten Island. Putting the cursor on the bars you can see the borough, the district number and statistical data according to the chosen criteria.



The data of these graphs are organized by the following three criteria:

1) Distances: This graph shows the distance between the center of each district and the NYU.

2) Crimes: In this graph you can see the number of crimes that occurred in each district.

3) Afford: This graph indicates the number of low-priced houses available in the different districts.



At the bottom you can see three buttons with the three criteria explained above, press them to change the graph and observe the data.

Map options



In this map the five boroughs of New York are drawn. In blue you have Manhattan, in purple to the Bronx, in red to Brooklyn, in yellow to Queens and finally in green to Staten Island. Also the districts of each borough are divided, you can put the cursor on the district and the area covered by the district will be shaded, besides if you click on the district a window containing the information of the district will be displayed, such as:

-Borough

-District number

-Distance to NYU

-Affordability

-Neighborhoods



In addition to these data, the map has four different selectors, these selectors will draw on the map the top 10 of the chosen criteria, in the lower part you can see the data of these districts in a table, this table will change data as criteria change. These selectors indicate:

1) Closeness: The 10 (high), 20 (medium) and 30 (low) best districts will be shown in terms of proximity to the NYU.

2) Security: The 10 (high), 20 (medium) and 30 (low) best districts will be shown in terms of security.

3) Affordability: The 10 (high), 20 (medium) and 30 (low) best districts will be shown in terms of affordability.

4) Top mixed: This selector will choose the first 10 districts mixing criteria between proximity (C), security (S) and affordability (A).
